Sticky Mango rice Recipe

Description

I first discovered this dish in the most unexpected place: an airport in Thailand.
I was tired, in-between flights, and not expecting anything more than the usual bland terminal food.
But then I spotted a small stall selling something wrapped in banana leaves; warm, fragrant, and slightly sweet-smelling.

Sticky mango rice is a sweet, creamy Thai dessert made with glutinous rice, ripe mango, and rich coconut milk.
Traditionally served warm in banana leaves, it's a tropical treat full of flavor and comfort.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup glutinous (sticky) rice
  • 1 ½ cups coconut milk
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 or 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced
  • Banana leaves (optional, for wrapping and presentation)
  • Sesame seeds or mung beans (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Soak the rice
    Rinse the sticky rice several times until the water runs clear.
    Soak in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  2. Cook the rice
    Drain the soaked rice and steam it for 20–30 minutes until tender.
    (Use a steamer lined with cheesecloth or a bamboo basket if possible.)

  3. Make the coconut sauce
    In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt.
    Heat gently until the sugar dissolves; don’t let it boil!

  4. Combine rice and sauce
    Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a bowl and slowly mix in about 1 cup of the warm coconut sauce.
    Let it sit for 10–15 minutes so the rice absorbs the flavor.

  5. Serve
    Shape the sticky rice into small portions (or roll into sticks if using banana leaves),
    top with mango slices, and drizzle with remaining coconut sauce.
    Garnish if desired.
