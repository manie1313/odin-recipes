Watermelon Gazpacho

Description

Watermelon gazpacho is a refreshing twist on the classic Spanish cold soup, blending juicy watermelon with tomatoes, cucumber, pepper, and a splash of olive oil.
Light, chilled, and perfect for hot days.

This version with watermelon adds a subtle sweetness that makes it even more special.

Ingredients(4 to 6 portions)

  • 1 liter (4 cups) seedless watermelon, cut into large cubes
  • 500 ml (2 cups) ripe tomatoes, peeled and cut into large cubes
  • 2 teaspoons sherry vinegar
  • Tabasco, to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 125 ml (1/2 cup) watermelon, finely diced (brunoise)
  • 125 ml (1/2 cup) Lebanese cucumber, unpeeled and finely diced (brunoise)
  • Fresh basil or mint leaves

STEPS (preparation: 15mn )

  1. Blend the base
    In a blender, combine the large cubes of watermelon and the peeled tomatoes.
    Blend until smooth and uniform in texture.



  2. Season the gazpacho
    Add sherry vinegar, a few dashes of Tabasco, salt, and pepper.
    Blend again briefly to combine.



  3. Strain (optional)
    If you prefer a smoother texture, strain the mixture through a fine sieve.
    Otherwise, skip this step for a more rustic feel.



  4. Chill
    Transfer the gazpacho to a bowl or pitcher and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
    This allows the flavors to develop and the soup to cool fully.



  5. Add diced garnish
    Just before serving, stir in the finely diced watermelon and cucumber (brunoise).



  6. Garnish and serve
    Pour into bowls and top with fresh basil or mint leaves.
    Serve cold and enjoy!
