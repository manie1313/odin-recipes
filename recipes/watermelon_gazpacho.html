Watermelon Gazpacho

Description

I love gazpacho in the summer — it’s refreshing, easy to make, and full of flavor.
This version with watermelon adds a subtle sweetness that makes it even more special.

Watermelon gazpacho is a refreshing twist on the classic Spanish cold soup, blending juicy watermelon with tomatoes, cucumber, pepper, and a splash of olive oil.
Light, chilled, and perfect for hot days.

Ingredients(4 to 6 portions)

  • 1 liter (4 cups) seedless watermelon, cut into large cubes
  • 500 ml (2 cups) ripe tomatoes, peeled and cut into large cubes
  • 2 teaspoons sherry vinegar
  • Tabasco, to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 125 ml (1/2 cup) watermelon, finely diced (brunoise)
  • 125 ml (1/2 cup) Lebanese cucumber, unpeeled and finely diced (brunoise)
  • Fresh basil or mint leaves

STEPS (preparation: 15mn )

  1. Step 1: Soak the rice
    Rinse the sticky rice several times until the water runs clear.
    Soak in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.



  2. Step 2: Cook the rice
    Drain the soaked rice and steam it for 20–30 minutes until tender.
    (Use a steamer lined with cheesecloth or a bamboo basket if possible.)



  3. Step 3: Make the coconut sauce
    In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt.
    Heat gently until the sugar dissolves; don’t let it boil!



  4. Step 4: Combine rice and sauce
    Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a bowl and slowly mix in about 1 cup of the warm coconut sauce.
    Let it sit for 10–15 minutes so the rice absorbs the flavor.



  5. Step 5: Serve
    Shape the sticky rice into small portions (or roll into sticks if using banana leaves),
    top with mango slices, and drizzle with remaining coconut sauce.
    Garnish if desired.
