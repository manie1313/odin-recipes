Sesame Noodles

Description

The first time I tried sesame noodles was at Din Tai Fung in Los Angeles.
I stopped in for dumplings, but ended up ordering a cold sesame noodle dish that completely stole the show.
It was simple but unforgettable — creamy, nutty, and full of flavor. Ever since that day, I’ve been hooked.

Sesame noodles are a rich and satisfying dish made with tender noodles tossed in a silky sesame-peanut sauce, soy, garlic, and a hint of spice.
Served cold or at room temperature, they’re quick, bold, and comforting.
Here is the recipe I got inspired by.

Ingredients (2 persons)

  • Dry Chinese wheat or egg noodles, boiled until al dente
  • 3 Tbsp sesame paste
  • 3 Tbsp creamy peanut butter (natural if possible)
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp white rice vinegar (optional)
  • 1-2 cloves of garlic, grated or minced
  • 1/4 thumb of ginger, grated or minced
  • 5-7 Tbsp of water, or as needed until sauce is runny
  • chopped spring onions and chili oil for garnish

STEPS (10mn preparation)

  1. Cook the Noodles
    Boil egg noodles around 3-4 minutes or until al dente.
    Rinse with cold water and set aside.



  2. Prepare the sauce
    Mix sesame paste, peanut butter, soy sauce, vinegar, minced ginger and garlic.
    Add water to the sauce until it’s runny; about the consistency of a creamy soup.



  3. Serve
    Pour sauce over noodles, garnish with spring onions and chili oil.
